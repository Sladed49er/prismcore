# Prism Core — Why We're Building This

*Living document. Started 2026-05-16. Updated as the product evolves — this is the
narrative spine for the June 3 PIA conference and everything after.*

---

## Start with WHY

Independent insurance agencies are trapped. There are ~40,000 of them in the U.S.,
and that number hasn't moved in 30 years — they can't grow, so they cannibalize each
other's clients. They are forced to run their business on software that was never
built for them.

We believe software should fit the business — not the business contorting itself to
fit the software.

## The problem (what's broken today)

- **Bloated platforms.** Agencies buy Vertafore / AMS360 / Applied and use ~5% of the
  features. They pay for all of it.
- **Overlapping products.** They bolt on a second and third product to fill gaps,
  often re-buying features they already pay for elsewhere.
- **Integration extortion.** Applied charges a ~$7,500/yr SDK license just to open an
  API. The development cost was recovered 15 years ago. It's a toll booth.
- **Transition dread.** Switching a CRM or VoIP system means downtime, data loss risk,
  and an "integration campaign" nobody wants to run. So agencies stay stuck.
- **Convoluted by design.** AMS platforms are convoluted because they try to be
  everything to everyone. They have to account for every possible case.

## HOW — the Prism Core approach

We don't try to account for every case. We give agencies the **pieces** and the
**scaffolding** to build exactly what they need.

- **The core is the composer.** The product is not a CRM. The core is the ability to
  compose software from modular pieces. The CRM is just one module among many.
- **Pick only what you need.** A software picker at onboarding: Do you need a CRM?
  VoIP integration? Document processing? Pick, and a tenant spins up with exactly that.
- **Customize it yourself.** Self-service, no-code. Add a field, build a form, wire a
  workflow — the way Kim rebuilt the PIA Western site herself.
- **Open APIs, no tolls.** We take in any API. No licensing fee for API access. No
  per-call charges. You pay for the service, per user, per module — right-sized.
- **Siloed tenants, shared core.** Every tenant is fully isolated with its own
  customizations, on shared base code. Security and zero-day patches push centrally,
  like OS updates.
- **The clearinghouse.** Every carrier API we add joins a growing pool. It becomes a
  marketplace — an agency can find "specialty markets for X in Oregon" and connect.
  Carriers and MGAs get reach (the prism analogy: one light in, a full spectrum out).

## WHAT

**Prism Core.** Software your way. Developer-as-a-Service — the tools and scaffolding
to make software do what you actually want, without paying for what you don't.

## The economics

A third the cost, twice the productivity. Right-sized per-user / per-module pricing —
agencies pay only for what they use. Everyone wins: they pay less for a better
product; we earn our margin on volume. It is "the opposite of every experience they
have right now."

## Proof it works (today)

- **PrismAMS** is live with paying customers (PIA Western — Kim).
- **CallIntel** is live with paying customers (Mitchell Reed).
- Kim — formerly anti-AI — now self-services her own site and forms, and moved her
  forms off Growth Zone ($11k/yr) into software she controls.

## June 3 — PIA conference
